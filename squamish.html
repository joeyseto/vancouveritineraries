Activities

What to Expect

1
~30 min
Coffee in Horseshoe Bay
☕
Small waterfront village at the edge of Vancouver where ferries depart for Vancouver Island. A peaceful place to grab coffee, watch boats come and go, and transition from city to highway scenery.
✦ Our tip
Go early in the morning to avoid ferry traffic backups and get the calmest views.
📍 Directions
2
30–60 min
Porteau Cove Stopover
📸
A quiet provincial park right on Howe Sound with dramatic mountain-and-ocean views. On clear days, the reflections on the water are unreal, and you can often see divers or campers along the shore.
✦ Our tip
Best for photos at sunrise or sunset — one of the most photogenic stops on the drive.
📍 Directions
3
20–30 min
Britannia Beach Coffee Stop
☕
A historic mining town along the highway with a few café stops and oceanfront views. A quick but interesting break between Vancouver and Squamish.
✦ Our tip
Grab coffee and a pastry and don't rush — the drive itself is part of the experience.
📍 Directions
4
30–45 min
Shannon Falls
💦
One of BC's tallest waterfalls, cascading down a dramatic granite cliff just off the highway. A short forest walk brings you right to the base where you can feel the spray.
✦ Our tip
Parking fills extremely fast — arrive early or visit midweek if possible.
📍 Directions
5
1–2 hours
Squamish Downtown Walk (Cleveland Ave)
🏔️
Squamish's main street is a relaxed mix of local cafés, outdoor shops, breweries, and bakeries, all framed by surrounding mountains. It feels very 'outdoor town meets creative hub.'
✦ Our tip
Sunflower Bakery Café is a great stop for coffee and pastries before exploring.
📍 Directions
6
Optional · 2–4 hours
Stawamus Chief Hike (Optional)
🧗
A steep but iconic hike up a massive granite monolith overlooking Howe Sound. The view from the top shows Squamish, mountains, and ocean all in one frame.
✦ Our tip
Start early — it gets hot fast and the trail is very exposed.
📍 Directions
7
Optional · 2–3 hours
Sea to Sky Gondola (Optional)
🚠
A scenic gondola ride that climbs into alpine terrain with suspension bridges, lookout platforms, and easy trails. The most accessible way to get 'mountain summit' views without a long hike.
✦ Our tip
Better for families or a relaxed pace — the views rival the Chief with far less effort.
📍 Directions
8
1–1.5 hours
Watershed Grill — Lunch or Dinner
🍽️
A riverside restaurant tucked into the forest along the Squamish River. Known for relaxed patio dining with a true 'Pacific Northwest lodge' feel.
✦ Our tip
Ask to sit outside if the weather is good.
📍 Directions
