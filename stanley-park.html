1
2–4 hours
Stanley Park
🌲
Start your day in Canada's largest urban park. Walk or bike portions of the famous Seawall (<10 km full loop), explore old-growth forest trails, and stop at viewpoints overlooking Burrard Inlet, Lions Gate Bridge, and the North Shore Mountains. Popular stops include Prospect Point, Brockton Point Totem Poles, Beaver Lake, and Third Beach.
✦ Our tip
If you're short on time, start from the parking lot around the Totem Poles, walk south to the Seawall Path, then walk clockwise toward Prospect Point. You'll see some of the park's best scenery with much less walking.

Optional · 1.5–2 hours
Vancouver Aquarium (Optional)
🐟
Located within Stanley Park, Vancouver's Aquarium features marine life from the Pacific Northwest and around the world. Interactive exhibits and rescued marine animals make it especially popular with families.
✦ Our tip
Easy to combine with Stanley Park since you're already inside the park. Families often spend longer here than expected.
📍 Directions
3
Flexible
Lunch along Denman or Robson Street
📍
Pick a restaurant along Denman or Robson — both offer a wide variety of dining options ranging from casual cafés and ramen shops to seafood restaurants and patios.
✦ Our tip
Denman Street is the more natural continuation from Stanley Park, while Robson offers more shopping and people-watching.
📍 Directions
4
Flexible
Rain or Shine Ice Cream (English Bay)
🍦
Rain or Shine is a local artisan ice cream shop known for small-batch flavours made with local ingredients. Their English Bay location is a short walk from the waterfront.
✦ Our tip
Our favourite flavour is the Malted Milk Chocolate Honeycomb. Expect a lineup on sunny weekends.
📍 Directions
5
2 hours
English Bay & Sunset Beach
🏖️
Finish your day relaxing along Vancouver's waterfront. Walk the seawall between English Bay and Sunset Beach, watch paddleboarders and kayakers, or simply enjoy the mountain views. Both beaches have a slightly different atmosphere and are both worth exploring.
✦ Our tip
There's a reason they call it Sunset Beach. If the weather cooperates, this is one of the best places in Vancouver to watch the sunset.
